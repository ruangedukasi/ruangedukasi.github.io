Jawaban:

Change these sentences into inversion.

1. My mother has never visited me since last year.

Never has my mother visited me since last year.

2. He rarely finished his work on time when he was young.

Rarely did he finish his work on time when he was young.

3. You will be able to finish your project only by making an accurate plan.

Be able will  you to finish your project only by making an accurate plan.

4. Erna has hardly done her activities based on the schedule.

Hardly has Erna done her activities based on the schedule.

5. They never write letters to their families.

Never do they write letters to their families.

6. I rarely come late in attending the lecture.

Rarely do I come late in attending the lecture.

7. He scarcely gives money for his children.

Scarcely does he gives money for his children.

8. They rarely join the outing programs conducted by their schools.

Rarely do they join the outing programs conducted by their schools.

9. He will never stop doing bad activities in his life.

Never will he stop doing bad activities in his life.

10. She hardly goes out alone.

Hardly does she go out alone.

Penjelasan:

Inversion just means putting the verb before the subject. In most English verb tenses, when we want to use inversion, we just move the verb to before the subject. If there's more than one verb, because a verb tense has auxiliary verbs for example, we move the first verb.

(+) I visited some great places

(-) I did not (didn't) visit some great places

(?) Did I visit some great places?

(+) I went to the airport and will fly to Cleveland

(-) I did not (didn't) go to the airport and will not (won't) fly to Cleveland

(?) Did I go to the airport and will fly to Cleveland?

(+) I spent three days there

(-) I did not (didn't) spend three days there

(?) Did I spend three days there?

4)

(+) I went to Hollywood

(-) I did not (didn't) go to Hollywood

(?) Did I go to Hollywood?

5)

(+) I was so amazing

(-) I was not (wasn't) so amazing

(?) Was it so amazing?

(+) My house was in the middle of the city

(-) My house was not (wasn't) in the middle of the city

(?) Was my house in the middle of the city?

(+) Parangtritis was a beautiful beach

(-) Parangtritis was not (wasn't) a beautiful beach

(?) Was Parangtritis a beautiful beach?

(+) There were some cloud on the sky

(+) We bought coconuts for the drink

10)

(+) We walked on the beach and touched the water
